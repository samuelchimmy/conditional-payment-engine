# Tether Developers Cup 🏆
## Build on Tether. Play like a knockout tournament.

The Tether Developers Cup is a global, football-themed developer competition that runs like a knockout tournament. Build a real-world application inspired by or designed for fans, clubs, players, coaches, stadiums, tournaments, or communities, think AI-powered coaching assistants, football analytics, prediction markets, fan engagement platforms, ticketing and payments, fantasy football, local tournament management, or something entirely new. Surprise us.

Teams represent a nation of their choice and compete through two elimination phases: a first submission on July 8 and a resubmission on July 12. The last 4 finalists pitch live to the Tether team, and winners are announced on July 19. There is 8,000 USDt in prizes. Please read the full rules and key dates below before you register.

---

## Key Dates (times in GMT-7)

* **Registration & Free Building**: Open through July 6. No cuts yet, everyone is building.
* **July 6**: Registration closes and the field locks.
* **July 8 (First Cut)**: Everyone must submit their project with a demo by July 8. Judges select the top 16 to move on and qualify for prizes. A resubmission then opens, so you can keep building.
* **July 12 (Second Cut - Semifinals)**: The field goes from 16 to 4 finalists. A resubmission opens again.
* **July 14, 23:59 (GMT-7)**: Final submission deadline. Finalists lock their build. Building stops.
* **July 15 to 18**: The 4 finalists pitch live to the Tether team.
* **July 19**: Winners announced.

Please read the Rules and Terms of Participation carefully before deciding if you want to participate. You are required to agree to both the Rules and Terms of Participation in order to participate in this Hackathon. The Rules and Terms of Participation should be read in conjunction with the Privacy Statement for the Hackathon.

## What You Can Build

Pick one or any combination of multiple tracks:

### 1. Pears Stack (P2P)
Peer-to-peer applications deployed with the Pear CLI and the pear-runtime library, built on the Pears Stack with building blocks such as Hyperswarm, Hypercore, and Autobase.
* **Docs**: [docs.pears.com](https://docs.pears.com)
* **Reference**: [Pears Building Blocks](https://docs.pears.com/reference/#building-blocks)
* **Ideas**: Match predictors, an AI coach or commentator, peer-to-peer fan messaging (no central server), group-tipping and ticket tools, watch-party sync.
* **Judged on**: Technical ambition, user experience, real-world utility, creativity, and real use of Pears.
* **Prize**: 🥇 1st place: 🟡 1,000 USD₮

### 2. QVAC (Local AI)
Apps that run AI models on the user's own device, with no cloud and no API keys, so data never leaves the machine. Language, speech, translation, or search.
* **Docs**: [qvac.tether.io](https://qvac.tether.io)
* **Ideas**:
  * Multi-agent systems with orchestration and tool calling.
  * Multimodal apps that combine vision, text, and audio.
  * Advanced RAG (retrieval-augmented generation: answering from your own documents) over large or personal collections.
  * Real-time apps that pair local inference with P2P delegation, where a phone offloads heavy work to a laptop or desktop you own.
  * Fine-tuning or LoRA adaptation with QVAC Fabric.
  * Privacy-first replacements for cloud apps: local note summarizers, translators, personal knowledge bases, support agents.
  * On-device personal assistants and health or wellness tools using QVAC MedPsy or other QVAC models.
  * Clever optimization that makes small models (1B to 3B) genuinely useful, even on low-power hardware like a Raspberry Pi.
* **Requirements**: All AI must run on the user's device through the QVAC SDK. No cloud AI. Runs on any device: laptop, desktop, phone, or single-board computer.
* **Judged on**: Technical ambition, user experience, real-world utility, creativity, and real use of QVAC.
* **Prize**: 🥇 1st place: 🟡 1,000 USD₮

### 3. WDK (Wallets)
Self-custodial wallet and payment apps, where the user holds their own keys, built with the Wallet Development Kit.
* **Docs**: [wdk.tether.io](https://wdk.tether.io)
* **Ideas**:
  * Everyday self-custodial apps: friendly wallets that make holding your own keys easy for normal people.
  * Tipping and creator payments: fan-to-creator tips, group tipping pools, or smart splits between creators and collaborators.
  * Programmable payments: subscriptions, escrow, milestone payouts, or event-triggered transfers.
  * Agent wallets: bots or AI agents that hold, send, and manage USDt, USAt, or XAUt on their own, using WDK primitives (wallet creation, signing, accounts) and an agent framework such as OpenClaw.
  * Autonomous finance: agents that lend, assess risk, manage debt, or deploy capital across DeFi (decentralized finance) protocols, settling on-chain in USDt, XAUt, or BTC.
* **Requirements**: Use WDK directly for wallet creation, signing, and accounts. Keep a clean separation between your app or agent logic and wallet execution. Think about permissions, spending limits, recovery, and role separation. Smart accounts or account abstraction are a plus.
* **Judged on**: Technical ambition, user experience, real-world utility, creativity, and real use of WDK.
* **Prize**: 🥇 1st place: 🟡 1,000 USD₮

## Prizes (8,000 USDt total)

* **Track Winners**: 1,000 USDt for the best build in each track (Pears, QVAC, WDK).
* **Cup Champion**: 5,000 USDt for the single best project across all tracks.
* All prizes are paid in USDt.

---

## Submission Requirements

1. **GitHub Repository**: A public GitHub repo with an open-source license (Apache 2.0 or MIT). Keep it public during the event and for a while after.
2. **Setup Instructions**: Clear setup steps so a judge can run it easily.
3. **Demo Video**: A demo video, 3 minutes max, on YouTube as "unlisted," with the link in your submission. Needed from the first submission on July 8.
4. **Prior Work Disclosure**: Tell us about any earlier work. You can reuse your own old code, but judges only score what you build during the event.

---

## How Projects Are Judged

A panel of Tether engineers and community experts scores each project from 1 to 5 on:
1. Technical ambition
2. User experience
3. Real-world use
4. Creativity
5. Real use of your track (or tracks)
